# HIRO Fitness Knowledge

## Philosophy

Fitness at HIRO is about sustainable, practical improvement — not extreme transformation. Members are busy adults 30+ balancing careers, families, and responsibilities. The goal is consistent progress that fits into real life.

## Core Principles

### Consistency Over Intensity
- 3-4 workouts per week is ideal for most members
- 30-45 minute sessions are effective and manageable
- Something is always better than nothing — a 15-minute walk counts

### Movement Quality First
- Proper form prevents injury and builds lasting results
- Master bodyweight movements before adding weight
- Mobility work is not optional — it's essential after 30

### Progressive Overload
- Small weekly increases in weight, reps, or time
- Track workouts to ensure progress
- Deload every 6-8 weeks to prevent burnout

## Recommended Training Splits

### Busy Professional (3x/week)
- Full body, each session
- Focus on compound movements
- 45 minutes per session

### Balanced Schedule (4x/week)
- Upper/Lower split
- 2 upper, 2 lower days
- 35-40 minutes per session

### Time-Efficient (2x/week)
- Full body both days
- Superset format to save time
- Focus on quality over quantity

## Key Exercises

### Foundation Movements
- Goblet squats (easier on back than barbell)
- Push-ups and variations
- Dumbbell rows
- Romanian deadlifts
- Walking lunges
- Pallof press (core stability)

### Mobility Essentials
- Hip flexor stretches
- Thoracic spine rotations
- Shoulder dislocates with band
- Ankle mobility drills
- Cat-cow flow

## Common Member Challenges

### "I don't have time"
- Morning workouts before the day starts
- 20-minute high-density sessions
- Lunch break movement breaks
- Walking meetings

### "I'm too tired after work"
- Morning routine is better for consistency
- Pre-workout nutrition matters
- Sleep quality directly impacts energy
- Start with 2x/week and build up

### "I don't know what I'm doing"
- HIRO provides structured programs
- Start with bodyweight foundations
- Video form checks available
- Community support for questions

### "I'm not seeing results"
- Nutrition is 70% of body composition
- Track measurements, not just weight
- Progress photos tell the real story
- 8-12 weeks for visible changes

## Recovery

### Sleep
- 7-8 hours is non-negotiable
- Consistent sleep/wake times
- Cool, dark room for optimal sleep

### Nutrition for Recovery
- Protein within 2 hours of training
- Hydration throughout the day
- Anti-inflammatory foods (berries, fatty fish, turmeric)

### Active Recovery
- Daily walks (8-10k steps)
- Light stretching on off days
- Foam rolling 2-3x/week

## Age-Specific Considerations (30+)

- Longer warm-ups needed (8-10 minutes)
- Joint health becomes a priority
- Recovery takes longer than in 20s
- Testosterone-supporting practices matter
- HIIT should be limited to 1-2x/week
