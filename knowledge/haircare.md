# HIRO Haircare Knowledge

## Philosophy

Hair health is part of the complete transformation picture. Whether the goal is maintaining healthy hair, addressing thinning, or optimizing growth, HIRO takes an evidence-based approach without gimmicks or false promises.

## Core Principles

### Scalp Health is Hair Health
- Healthy hair starts at the scalp
- Regular cleansing prevents buildup that clogs follicles
- Scalp massage increases blood flow
- Treat scalp issues (dandruff, inflammation) promptly

### Gentle Handling
- Wet hair is fragile — be gentle when towel-drying
- Wide-tooth comb for detangling, not a brush
- Avoid tight hairstyles that cause tension
- Heat protection is non-negotiable before styling

### Nutrition Matters
- Hair is non-essential tissue — the body prioritizes it last
- Protein deficiency shows in hair first
- Iron, zinc, vitamin D, and biotin support hair health
- Crash diets cause temporary hair shedding

## Hair Loss and Thinning (Male Pattern)

### First-Line Approaches
- Minoxidil (topical): FDA-approved, effective for crown and diffuse thinning
- Finasteride (oral, prescription): blocks DHT, effective but requires doctor
- Ketoconazole shampoo (Nizoral): mild anti-androgen effect
- Start early — prevention is easier than regrowth

### Supportive Practices
- Scalp massage 5-10 minutes daily (increases blood flow)
- Microneedling (dermaroller) 1x/week with 1.5mm (use with minoxidil for better results)
- Reduce scalp inflammation with gentle products
- Avoid harsh chemical treatments

### What Doesn't Work
- Biotin supplements (unless deficient — rare)
- Caffeine shampoos (no meaningful evidence)
- "Natural" DHT blockers (saw palmetto has very weak evidence)
- Special pillows or laser combs (weak evidence for most)

## Hair Loss and Thinning (Female Pattern)

- Diffuse thinning rather than receding hairline
- Often related to hormonal changes, stress, or nutritional gaps
- Minoxidil is first-line for women too (2% or 5%)
- Iron and vitamin D levels should be checked
- Gentle styling — avoid tension and excessive heat

## Daily Care

### Washing Frequency
- Oily scalp: daily or every other day
- Normal scalp: every 2-3 days
- Dry/curly hair: 1-2x per week
- Use dry shampoo between washes sparingly

### Product Selection
- Sulfate-free shampoos are gentler
- Silicone-free conditioners for fine hair
- Protein treatments for damaged hair
- Leave-in conditioner for dry or curly hair

### Styling
- Heat protectant always before hot tools
- Lowest effective heat setting
- Air dry when possible
- Silk/satin pillowcase reduces friction and breakage

## Lifestyle Factors

### Stress
- Telogen effluvium: stress-induced shedding (temporary, 3-6 months)
- Chronic stress impacts hair growth cycle
- Stress management supports hair health

### Sleep
- Growth hormone released during deep sleep
- 7-8 hours for optimal recovery
- Silk pillowcase protects hair overnight

### Exercise
- Improves circulation including to scalp
- Reduces stress (lowers cortisol)
- Sweat can irritate scalp — rinse after intense workouts

## When to See a Specialist

- Sudden patchy hair loss
- Scarring or redness on scalp
- Hair loss with other symptoms (fatigue, weight changes)
- No response to over-the-counter treatments after 6 months
- Consider a dermatologist who specializes in hair disorders
