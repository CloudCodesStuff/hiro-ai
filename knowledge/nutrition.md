# HIRO Nutrition Knowledge

## Philosophy

Nutrition at HIRO is about fueling your best self without obsession. No extreme diets, no demonizing foods, no guilt. Just practical, evidence-based strategies that work for busy adults.

## Core Principles

### Protein Priority
- Aim for 1.6-2.0g per kg of bodyweight daily
- Distribute protein across 3-4 meals
- Prioritize whole food sources: lean meats, fish, eggs, dairy, legumes
- Protein supports muscle maintenance, especially important after 30

### Whole Food Foundation
- 80% of diet from minimally processed foods
- Colorful vegetables at most meals
- Quality carbohydrates for energy
- Healthy fats for hormone function

### Flexible Approach
- 20% flexibility for social events, treats, dining out
- No forbidden foods
- Focus on what to add, not what to remove
- Sustainability over perfection

## Meal Structure

### Breakfast (within 2 hours of waking)
- Protein + complex carb + fruit/vegetable
- Example: Greek yogurt with berries and oats
- Example: Eggs with whole grain toast and avocado

### Lunch
- Lean protein + vegetables + quality carb
- Example: Grilled chicken salad with quinoa
- Example: Salmon with roasted vegetables

### Dinner
- Protein + vegetables + optional carb based on activity level
- Example: Lean beef stir-fry with brown rice
- Example: White fish with sweet potato and greens

### Snacks (as needed)
- Protein-focused: cottage cheese, hard-boiled eggs, protein shake
- Energy-focused: apple with almond butter, trail mix
- Recovery-focused: chocolate milk, protein bar

## Hydration

- Minimum 2-3 liters of water daily
- More with exercise or hot weather
- Start morning with 500ml water
- Herbal teas count toward hydration
- Limit alcohol — it impacts sleep, recovery, and progress

## Supplements (Evidence-Based Only)

### Recommended
- Vitamin D3 (especially if low sun exposure)
- Omega-3 fatty acids (fish oil or algae)
- Magnesium (glycinate form for sleep)
- Creatine monohydrate (5g daily, benefits beyond muscle)
- Protein powder (convenience, not necessity)

### Not Recommended
- Fat burners (ineffective, often dangerous)
- Testosterone boosters (no evidence for OTC products)
- BCAAs (whole protein is better)
- Detox teas (marketing, not science)

## Eating Out Strategy

### Restaurant Guidelines
- Protein + vegetables as the base of your order
- Ask for dressings and sauces on the side
- Don't fear asking for modifications
- Enjoy the meal — one dinner won't undo progress

### Social Events
- Eat normally before the event
- Choose what you truly want, skip what you don't
- Alcohol: 1-2 drinks max, water between each
- Return to normal eating next meal — no compensation needed

## Common Challenges

### "I eat well but can't lose weight"
- Track portions for 1-2 weeks to identify blind spots
- Liquid calories add up (coffee drinks, alcohol, juices)
- Sleep and stress impact fat loss significantly
- Consider a modest calorie deficit (300-500 calories)

### "I'm always hungry"
- Increase protein at each meal
- Add more fiber (vegetables, legumes, whole grains)
- Check hydration — thirst mimics hunger
- Eat more volume foods (vegetables, berries, soups)

### "Meal prep takes too long"
- Batch cook proteins for 3-4 days
- Pre-cut vegetables for quick assembly
- Keep healthy convenience options (rotisserie chicken, frozen vegetables, canned beans)
- 15-minute meals are completely viable
