# HIRO Lifestyle Knowledge

## Philosophy

True transformation goes beyond fitness and nutrition. HIRO helps members optimize all aspects of their lifestyle — sleep, stress, confidence, grooming, and daily routines — to create lasting change.

## Sleep Optimization

### Why Sleep Matters
- Recovery happens during sleep (muscle repair, hormone production)
- Poor sleep increases cortisol (stress hormone) and hunger hormones
- Decision-making and willpower decline with sleep deficit
- Consistent poor sleep undermines every other health effort

### The HIRO Sleep Protocol
1. Consistent wake time (even on weekends)
2. Morning sunlight exposure (10-15 minutes within 30 min of waking)
3. No caffeine after 2 PM
4. Screen curfew: 60 minutes before bed (or blue-light blocking glasses)
5. Cool room: 65-68°F (18-20°C)
6. Dark room: blackout curtains or sleep mask
7. Wind-down routine: reading, light stretching, meditation

### Common Sleep Disruptors
- Alcohol: helps fall asleep but destroys sleep quality
- Late meals: digestion interferes with deep sleep
- Inconsistent schedule: circadian rhythm needs regularity
- Phone in bedroom: temptation and blue light
- Caffeine sensitivity increases with age

## Stress Management

### Understanding Stress
- Acute stress is normal and adaptive
- Chronic stress is the enemy of transformation
- Cortisol promotes fat storage (especially abdominal)
- Stress impairs recovery, sleep, and decision-making

### Practical Stress Tools
- Morning routine (even 10 minutes of quiet time)
- Breathwork: 4-7-8 breathing (inhale 4s, hold 7s, exhale 8s)
- Daily walk without phone/podcasts (mental reset)
- Journaling: 5 minutes of brain dump before bed
- Setting boundaries: learning to say no
- Professional support when needed (therapy is maintenance, not crisis-only)

### Work-Life Balance (For Busy Professionals)
- Define non-negotiable personal time
- Batch similar tasks to reduce context switching
- Take actual lunch breaks away from desk
- Protect workout time like a meeting
- Delegate when possible — at work and at home

## Grooming and Presentation

### Daily Grooming Baseline
- Clean, well-moisturized skin
- Maintained facial hair (clean shaven or well-groomed beard)
- Clean, trimmed nails
- Fresh, appropriate fragrance (less is more)
- Well-fitted clothes make more impact than expensive clothes

### Elevating Your Presentation
- Find a consistent haircut schedule (every 3-4 weeks)
- Invest in quality basics: well-fitting jeans, clean white shirt, good shoes
- Posture communicates confidence before you speak
- Eye contact and a genuine smile are free and powerful
- Dress for how you want to feel, not just the occasion

## Confidence Building

### Internal Work
- Confidence comes from keeping promises to yourself
- Set and achieve small daily goals (momentum)
- Track wins, not just gaps
- Comparison is the thief of progress — compare to your past self
- Competence breeds confidence — invest in skills

### External Practices
- Posture: shoulders back, head up
- Speak slightly slower than feels natural
- Prepare for important conversations and meetings
- Dress intentionally for confidence-boosting situations
- Physical fitness directly impacts self-perception

## Morning Routine Template

### The HIRO Morning (30-45 minutes)
1. Wake at consistent time
2. Hydrate: 500ml water immediately
3. Sunlight exposure: 10 minutes outside or by window
4. Movement: walk, stretch, or workout
5. Mindfulness: 5 minutes meditation or journaling
6. Protein-rich breakfast
7. Review top 3 priorities for the day

### Evening Wind-Down
1. Last meal 2-3 hours before bed
2. Dim lights after sunset
3. Screen-free activity (reading, conversation, hobby)
4. Prepare for tomorrow (clothes, meals, schedule)
5. Gratitude: note 3 things that went well today
6. Consistent bedtime

## Relationships and Social Health

- Strong relationships are a top predictor of happiness and longevity
- Schedule quality time with important people
- Be present: phone away during conversations
- Men especially benefit from maintaining close friendships
- Community (like HIRO) provides accountability and support
